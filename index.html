02 About
A note from Kiran

I treat organic search as a product surface — measurable, testable, and ruthlessly optimised against real revenue.

My background sits at the seam between SEO strategy and applied data engineering. I started as a support engineer in banking and HR systems — writing SQL, automating thousands of manual workflows — before moving to Berlin for a Master's in Digital Engineering and joining Wayfair, where I scaled from intern to Senior Product Analyst.

At Wayfair, I owned organic growth across multiple international markets, partnering with Engineering, Product, and Content to align organic visibility with revenue. I shipped ML-based sentence-transformer models for internal linking, built a Python AI system for multilingual content generation, and ran end-to-end experimentation programs — 15+ A/B tests, full statistical design through rollout decisions.

What I care about: scale without manual toil. Most of my work ends in an automated pipeline, a self-service tool for non-technical teams, or a dashboard that replaces a recurring meeting. I'm bullish on AI-driven search (GEO/LLM optimisation) as the next layer of the discipline — and I've been integrating OpenAI and Anthropic APIs into production SEO workflows since well before it was the default.

Outside of work I'm a hackathon person — runner-up at the Cyprus Hackathon for Workwise, an AI agent app — and I read widely on culture, travel, and how systems get built.
